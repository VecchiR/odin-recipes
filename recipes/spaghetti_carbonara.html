Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, bacon, and black pepper. It's a simple yet incredibly flavorful dish that's perfect for a quick and easy meal.

Ingredients

For the sauce

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper

For the pasta

  • 8 ounces spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 slices bacon, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley (optional)

Steps

Sauce

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs, Parmesan cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper. Set aside.

Pasta

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to package directions until al dente (usually about 8-10 minutes).
  2. Meanwhile, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the bacon and cook until crispy, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Remove the bacon from the pan with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  4. Add the garlic to the pan and cook for about 30 seconds, until fragrant.
  5. Immediately turn off the heat.

Combining

  1. Drain the pasta, reserving about 1/2 cup of the pasta water.
  2. Add the hot pasta to the skillet with the garlic and toss to coat.
  3. Working quickly, pour in the egg mixture and toss constantly until the sauce thickens and coats the pasta, about 30 seconds. If the sauce seems too thick, add a little of the reserved pasta water to thin it out.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the reserved bacon and parsley (if using).
  5. Serve immediately with additional grated Parmesan cheese and black pepper, if desired.

Tips

  • For an even richer flavor, you can use guanciale instead of bacon. Guanciale is cured pork cheek that has a deeper, more savory flavor than bacon.
  • If you don't have Parmesan cheese, you can use Pecorino Romano cheese.
  • Be careful not to overcook the eggs, or they will become scrambled.
  • This dish is best served immediately, as the sauce will continue to thicken as it cools.
